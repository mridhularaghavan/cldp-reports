Analysis of the CLDP Karnataka WhatsApp group

About the report

⚠ This report is intended for internal team use only. The source data contains phone numbers — please be mindful before sharing the source file externally.

1
How messages were grouped. Each person's messages sent within 5 minutes of each other were treated as a single exchange. A photo, its caption, and a follow-up note sent in quick succession count as one. This reduced 174 rows in the source data to 84 grouped exchanges through April 2026. A June 2026 refresh added a further 17 exchanges spanning late April to mid-June, for 101 exchanges in total, which form the basis of all figures in this report.
2
One message was excluded. A message from Feb 2026 was identified as forwarded content unrelated to the group's purpose. It received no engagement and is not counted in any analysis.
3
Photos and media are read in context. Media files cannot be opened from a WhatsApp export. Where a photo was sent alongside a caption or text in the same exchange, the text was used to understand what the media was about. Photos sent with no text at all are noted but not categorised.

Source data: 101 clustered message exchanges  ·  Report generated on 23 April 2026  ·  Updated 13 June 2026 — extended through June 2026

Note: The May–June 2026 update is reflected in Month-on-Month Progress and Poll Analysis. The Overview split and the individual sender leaderboard reflect the original Nov 2025–Apr 2026 dataset, as the refresh export covered only the later months.

Key themes

Key themes

Based on councillor messages only

Roads and Drains

Updates on construction and inspection of roads, drain slabs, concrete works, and asphalting across wards in Hebbagodi, Chandapura, Doddaballapur and Bandenallasandra. The most dominant theme — over a third of all councillor activity.

CLDP Programme Engagement

Councillors confirming certificate receipt, sharing council term data in response to HL Sir's request, and expressing gratitude for the Delhi Leadership Conference — reflecting active participation in the programme itself.

Public Health

Active participation in the Pulse Polio campaign across multiple Hebbagodi wards, and a free health check-up programme for pregnant women at Vinayaka Nagar, Kammasandra (Ward 28).

Community Programmes

UGD inauguration in Chandapura Ward 23; yoga and tailoring classes launched in Jigani; wall painting awareness with Sahas NGO in Hebbagodi Ward 25.

MLA / MP Engagement

Meeting with Ramanagara MLA Iqbal Hussain; road safety inspection visit by Bengaluru Rural MP Dr. C.N. Manjunath at Hebbagodi on National Highway-44.

Waste Segregation

Councillors from Hebbagodi led citizen awareness on proper waste segregation through a wall painting programme and Garbage Kiosk booth visits.

Month-on-month

Month-on-month progress

Message clusters by month, split by sender type

Since the group's launch in November 2025, engagement fell sharply through the winter — but the May–June 2026 refresh shows a clear recovery. Activity peaked at launch (37 clusters), bottomed out in early 2026, then rebuilt through April–June as councillor field updates returned (a water-pipeline replacement, World Environment Day ward activity) and the 12 June quiz poll drew record participation (15 votes, 28.8%). The reversal suggests that interactive prompts — polls and quizzes in particular — re-activate the group more effectively than document drops.

Nov – Dec 2025

Peak engagement window. The group was new, the CLDP workshop was fresh, and HL Sir's structured questions (certificates, council terms) drove back-and-forth dialogue with all ULBs responding.

Jan – Feb 2026

Steep activity cliff — only 1–2 senders contributed. No structured prompts from Janagraha in January. February's educational content (Finance Commission PDF) received zero replies, suggesting the group needs interactive prompts rather than document drops to stay engaged.

March 2026

Modest recovery driven by the poll format and the Delhi conference follow-up. The poll alone generated 10 votes — the most interaction any single 2026 message produced, validating participatory formats as a re-activation strategy.

Apr – Jun 2026

Sustained recovery. Councillor-initiated field reporting returned — Sujatha Harish's water-infrastructure updates and the World Environment Day ward activity were the first substantive on-ground posts since December. The 12 June quiz built on the poll strategy and set a new participation high (15 votes, 28.8%), confirming that interactive formats, not document shares, drive this group.
