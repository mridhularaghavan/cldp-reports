About the report

⚠ This report is intended for internal team use only. The source data contains phone numbers — please be mindful before sharing the source file externally.

1
How messages were grouped. Each person's messages sent within 5 minutes of each other were treated as a single exchange. A photo, its caption, and a follow-up note sent in quick succession count as one. After removing system notices and deleted messages, the source export condenses to 14 grouped exchanges, which form the basis of all figures in this report.
2
Member count is based on visible participants only. Unlike other CLDP groups, this group has no phone-number join records in the export — all members appear by display name. The figure of 11 members reflects unique individuals who appear anywhere in the export (as senders, group creator, or in system messages). The actual group size may be larger.
3
Photos and media are read in context. Media files cannot be opened from a WhatsApp export. Where a photo was sent alongside a caption or text in the same exchange, the text was used to understand what the media was about. Photos sent with no text at all are noted but not categorised.

Key themes

Key themes

Based on participant messages — Jun 2026

General / Other

All 10 participant clusters in this period fall under General/Other. Seven were condolence responses on 11 Jun, following the passing of someone associated with the group — members across the country sent brief messages of solidarity within a few hours of each other. The remaining three were uncontextualised media posts from ~ Sekhar (on 11, 13, and 23 Jun) with no accompanying text.

CLDP Programme Engagement (admin-initiated)

The three admin clusters from Aarushi carried the programme engagement in this period. These included a quiz poll on project prioritisation (12 Jun), the reveal of the correct answer (18 Jun), and a discussion prompt asking participants to name one ward-level citizen priority they would advocate for in the next City Action Plan (19 Jun). The discussion prompt drew no visible text response from participants in the export period.

Notable gap: This group contains no participant-initiated programme content in the export period. All substantive engagement was admin-driven. No field updates, ward issues, or civic concerns were raised by participants.

Month-on-month

Month-on-month progress

Message clusters by month, split by sender type

The Cities Rising group was created on 3 July 2025 but remained completely silent for nearly eleven months. All 14 clusters in this export — the group's entire documented activity — took place in June 2026. The month opened with a burst of media from ~ Sekhar and an impromptu community condolence thread, before Aarushi introduced the programme's standard quiz poll and a substantive discussion prompt. It is not possible to describe a trend across months; this report captures a single active month from a group that is still in early activation.

The group's first active month. Activity fell into three distinct moments: a media burst and community condolence thread on 11 Jun (organically participant-led), a quiz poll cycle on 12–18 Jun (admin-led, drew 3 votes), and a discussion prompt on 19 Jun asking participants to name one ward-level citizen priority for the City Action Plan (admin-led, no visible text replies by 23 Jun). Taken together, the month suggests a group that is just beginning to activate, with participants willing to engage around personal/communal moments but not yet responding to programme prompts in writing.
