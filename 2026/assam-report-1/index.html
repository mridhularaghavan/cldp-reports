About the report

⚠ This report is intended for internal team use only. The source data contains phone numbers — please be mindful before sharing the source file externally.

1
How messages were grouped. Each person's messages sent within 5 minutes of each other were treated as a single exchange. A photo, its caption, and a follow-up note sent in quick succession count as one. After removing system notices, deleted messages and media-only repeats, the source export condenses to 19 grouped exchanges, which form the basis of all figures in this report.
2
Total membership could not be established. Almost everyone joined this group silently through a group-invite link, so the export contains no add/join records to count from. Figures in this report are therefore based on active participants — the 9 people who sent at least one message — and not on full group size.
3
Photos and media are read in context. Media files cannot be opened from a WhatsApp export. Where a photo was sent alongside a caption or text in the same exchange, the text was used to understand what the media was about. Photos sent with no text at all are noted but not categorised.

Key themes

Key themes

Based on councillor messages only

CLDP Programme Engagement

The dominant theme by far. Councillor activity centred on the programme itself — requesting and acknowledging participation certificates, asking for and thanking admins for the shared programme photos, and confirming which municipal board they represent. This is logistics-and-gratitude engagement rather than field reporting.

Community Programmes

Festival and civic-observance greetings shared by councillors — Bhogali Bihu wishes (with a photo) and a Happy Republic Day message (with a photo). These were one-way greetings that drew no further discussion.

Notable gap: Unlike Karnataka, this group contains no councillor updates on roads, drains, ward works or field activity. Councillor messages were limited to programme logistics and festival greetings across the full six-month period.

Month-on-month

Month-on-month progress

Message clusters by month, split by sender type

Since the group became active in January 2026, overall engagement has declined. Activity peaked in the launch month (9 clusters — driven by onboarding photos and festival greetings) and fell to low single digits from February onward, dominated by one-way Janaagraha educational posts. May 2026 was completely silent, and June saw only a small uptick around the 74th Amendment anniversary. The trend mirrors the wider CLDP pattern: early onboarding energy was not sustained, and without interactive prompts councillor activity tapered off.

January 2026

Launch-window peak. Onboarding drove most of the activity — admins sharing event photos and scheme documents, and councillors responding with festival greetings and thank-yous. This was the most participatory month of the whole period.

Feb – Apr 2026

Activity cliff. The February certificate exchange was the only genuine dialogue; March and April were carried almost entirely by Janaagraha educational posts (OSR, AMRUT 2.0) that drew no councillor replies. The OSR poll's 4 votes were the single largest piece of councillor interaction in this window.

May – Jun 2026

May was silent. June's small revival was admin-led — a 74th Amendment greeting and a photo — with just one councillor thank-you in response. Councillor-initiated field activity remained absent throughout.
